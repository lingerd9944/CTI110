Some brief information on some historically significant psychological experiments

These illustrate some of the tragic misteps that researchers have taken in the past. This is by no means an exhaustive list.
Experimenter Albert Bandura Kenneth Clark Philip Zimbardo Muzafer Sherif
Experiment Bobo Doll Doll Test Stanford Prison Robbers Cave
Year 1961 1940 1971 1961
Focus Aggression Stereotypes Authority Aggression Prejudice
Participants 37 to 69 months (72 participants) 3 and 5 yr old African American children (150 participants) College students (24 participants) 11 year old boys (22 particpants)
Method Children left to observe an aggressive or nonaggressive model and then left to model the behavior, plus a control group African American children asked to chose either a Black or White doll in relation to series of questions Randomly assign participants to guard or prisoner role and observe behavior and interactions of each group Boys sent to a camp. placed in groups, development of in-group/out-group mentality, resolution of intergroup conflict
Implications Children learn aggressive behavior through observation- Social Learning Theory African American children hold a negative stereotype about 'Blacks' and a positive outlook of 'Whites' Brutality imposed on prisoners by gaurds is not necessarily due to pre-existing personality types of the guards, but partially due to the position of power they are in. Limited resources and competition lead to prejudice and aggression- Realistic Conflict Theory
Criticisms Could be viewed as unethical to model aggressive behaviors to young children Lacks experimental structure, famous from the 1954 Supreme Court case Brown v Topeka Board of Education Highly unethical due to the brutality 'prisoners' faced at the hands of the 'guards' with little to no oversight intervention by researchers Focuses only on boys, of a similar socioeconomic status, ethnic identity, religious backgroud, thus ungeneralisable
